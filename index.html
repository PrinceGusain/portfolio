The Sound of Music – Movie Review

The Sound of Music is a beautiful and heartwarming movie set in Austria just before World War II. It tells the story of Maria, a cheerful and kind young woman training to be a nun. She's sent to be the governess for a strict naval officer’s seven children. At first, the kids are distant and the father is cold, but Maria slowly brings music, laughter, and love into their home. The movie has some really famous songs like "Do-Re-Mi," "My Favorite Things," and "Edelweiss." Julie Andrews, who plays Maria, is absolutely charming and makes you feel every moment. The movie is also visually stunning, with amazing views of the mountains and countryside. It’s not just about music and family—it also shows how the family stands up for what’s right when the Nazis take over Austria. It's a mix of joy, romance, and courage that still feels meaningful even today.

3 Simple Questions & Answers

Q1: What is The Sound of Music mainly about? A: It’s about a woman named Maria who brings love and music into a strict family’s life and helps them become close again.

Q2: How does Maria help the von Trapp family? A: She teaches the kids to sing and play, helps them open up emotionally, and brings the father and children closer together.

Q3: Why do the von Trapps leave Austria? A: Because Captain von Trapp doesn’t want to work for the Nazis, so the family escapes to stay free and safe.

Week 6 Assignment - Top 25 Learnings from PESE lectures

1. Don’t Make Fun of Classmates
Everyone is learning—respect helps us grow together.

2. We Are a Team
Supporting each other creates a positive space to improve.

3. Help Each Other Improve
Share tips, correct gently, and celebrate progress.

4. Learn from Each Other's Mistakes
Every mistake is a chance for the whole class to learn.

5. Practice English Daily
Spend at least 1 hour reading, writing, listening, or speaking in English.

6. Read to Build Vocabulary
Reading books, articles, or even comics helps you learn new words and sentence structures.

7. Write Regularly
Journaling, essays, or even short stories help improve grammar and expression.

8. Listen to English Every Day
Watch videos, listen to podcasts, or songs to improve pronunciation and understanding.

9. Speak Without Fear
Fluency comes from speaking, even if you make mistakes.

10. Group Activities Build Teamwork
Working together helps develop leadership, cooperation, and confidence.

11. Speeches Build Confidence
Speaking in front of others improves public speaking and self-expression.

12. Group Discussions Improve Thinking
Talking about topics helps develop opinions and listening skills.

13. Group Presentations Teach Responsibility
Everyone has a part, and success comes from working together.

14. Give and Receive Feedback Politely
Positive and respectful feedback helps improve performance.

15. Behave Well in Class
Discipline shows respect for the teacher, classmates, and learning process.

16. Be Respectful to All Opinions
Everyone sees things differently—learn to listen with an open mind.

17. Confidence Grows with Practice
The more you participate, the stronger your communication becomes.

18. Use English in Daily Life
Talk to friends, ask questions, and think in English to improve fluency.

19. Stay Positive and Encourage Others
A good attitude makes the whole class more motivated.

20. Correct Gently, Not Rudely
Help your friends improve without making them feel bad.

21. Don’t Be Afraid to Make Mistakes
Mistakes are part of learning—what matters is learning from them.

22. Be an Active Listener
Show that you are paying attention when someone is speaking.

23. Take Notes During Discussions
Writing down ideas helps remember and learn better.

24. Respect Everyone’s Speaking Time
Don’t interrupt—let everyone express themselves.

25. Celebrate Small Improvements
Growth takes time—recognize each step forward.
